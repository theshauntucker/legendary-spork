# About Deconstruction Aggregator

## Our Mission

Deconstruction Aggregator is a cross-religion knowledge base dedicated to understanding religious deconstruction, high-control groups, and recovery. We aggregate, organize, and summarize resources from academic research, survivor accounts, and expert analysis to help people navigate faith transitions and understand group dynamics.

We serve people at all stages: those questioning their faith, people in the process of leaving, those newly out, and family members trying to understand a loved one's experience.

## What We Do

This site curates resources across multiple religions and belief systems—from mainstream churches to independent cults—examining control mechanisms, trauma recovery, and paths to freedom. We believe informed, accessible information saves lives.

We cover:
- **High-control group dynamics** — understanding manipulation and undue influence
- **Religious trauma and recovery** — processing exits and healing
- **Specific religions and groups** — detailed resource libraries for each tradition
- **Practical support** — community, stories, and action guides

## Editorial Standards

Every resource on this site is:
1. **Linked to the original source** for verification and deep reading
2. **Summarized in fair use** — we extract key information to help you quickly find what matters
3. **Attributed to creators** — author, publication, date clearly stated
4. **Categorized and tagged** — organized for discovery and comparison

We don't replace primary sources. We make them easier to find and understand.

## How We're Funded

Deconstruction Aggregator is sustainable through:
- **Advertising** (AdSense, Mediavine, Monumetric, Raptive)
- **Affiliate commissions** on recommended therapy services (BetterHelp, Talkspace) and books (Amazon Associates)
- **Donations** via Ko-fi and Buy Me a Coffee

100% of advertising and affiliate revenue supports hosting, development, and content creation. We are fully independent and accept no donations from religious organizations or groups.
